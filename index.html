About

Iguig (Ibanag: Ili nat Iguig; Ilocano: Ili ti Iguig; Tagalog: Bayan ng Iguig), officially the Municipality of Iguig, is a 4th class municipality in the province of Cagayan, Philippines. According to the 2015 census, it has a population of 27,862 people. It is best known as the hometown of Dado Banatao, a well-known Filipino-American software engineer credited for having developed the first 10-Mbit Ethernet CMOS with silicon coupler data-link control and trans-receiver chip. The town of Iguig was founded by Rev. Ambrocio De La Madre De Dios, O.P. on December 28, 1607. This maker, attached by the Philippine Historical Commission to the façade of the parish church,attests to the antiquity of Iguig. The native name of the town is “IHIG” and tradition has it that it was the name of the chieftain of the village which the Spaniards made into a “pueblo” on December 28,1607 and the Dominicans accepted as a “mission” on April 16,1608 with Santiago Apostol as the Patron Saint.

It is said that in the early 1970’s, a team of local and foreign archaeologists uncovered fragments of fossilized ornaments and clay pots around various locations in the town of Iguig. Some believe these artifacts prove the existence of an ancient pottery industry that flourished hundreds or even a thousand years ago.

Church

The church’s location is another of its advantages. The centuries-old church is built on an elevated area, the Calvary Hills, which gives pilgrims an awesome view of the mighty Cagayan Valley River, the longest river in the country. There’s actually a brick stairway, which goes straight from the church property into the river. There’s also a lovely, old well within the church grounds that add a charming touch.

Tourism

Apart from the religious and cultural sites, Iguig's natural beauty may attract tourists interested in exploring the scenic landscapes and the Cagayan River. The region's rich biodiversity and the potential for outdoor activities might make it an appealing destination for nature enthusiasts.
